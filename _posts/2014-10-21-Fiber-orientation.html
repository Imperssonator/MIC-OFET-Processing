Identify fibers The thresholding post discusses our algorithm for finding the perfect thresholding value to isolate individual fibers. The following image shows a brief example of our work. ![thresholding algorithm vizulaized](https://farm6.staticflickr.com/5616/15408121609_eece533bef_b.jpg) The result of such thresholding will be used to identify each fiber as a connected component. This can be used to find individual fiber orientation. ##Ellipse fitting to find 2. Fiber orientation [regionprops](http://www.mathworks.com/help/images/ref/regionprops.html#bqkf8ik) was used to fit an ellipse to each connected component obtained from the previous thresholding to determine the orientation of that fiber. The following example is a proof of concept of finding orientations using regionprops. Of the AFM image(left) , we selected to only 5 components(center). The orientations of the components were accurately calculated and are displayed on the right. ![Ex. of orientation finding](https://farm6.staticflickr.com/5603/15594371682_1d8daa5de0_b.jpg) The orientation detection along with thresholding is displayed below. Each pixel in the image identified as a fiber can be assigned one of 8 orientations. ![Full process](https://farm4.staticflickr.com/3939/14974265374_329dc65684_b.jpg) ###Issues Overlapping fiber, especially those in completely different directions will contaminate the orientation calculation. The image below shows a case where the ellipse fit to the connected component in green will chose the green vector to calculate the orientation of the fiber. This orientation is incorrect for both fibers. ![incorrect orientation](https://farm6.staticflickr.com/5609/14974332554_0d084b46e6_o.png) ####Possible solution: Calculate the eccentricity of the ellipse fit the connected component and if the ellipse cannot possible belong to a single fiber, remove from orientation calculation. Use some other method to perform some sort of local orientation calculation. ##Peak fitting to find 3. Degree of crystallinity Expeimentaing with SciPy in Python.
